National Parks Facts

1. The first national park:
Yellowstone, established 1872.
2. The largest tree in the world:
"General Sherman", which is a single-stem tree 275 feet tall and weighing an estimated 1900 metric tons!
3. The largest national park:
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park, Alaska. It covers 13,000 square miles!
4. The state with the most national parks:
Trick question! California and Alaska both have the most, each state contains eight parks.
5. The least visited national park:
Kobuk Valley National Park, in Alaska.
6. The deepest lake in the US:
Crater Lake in Oregon is 1932 feet deep!
7. National parks conservation effects:
Over 400 endangered or threatened plant and animal species are given home and habitat in the National Parks.
8. The only state without National Parks or National Monuments:
Poor Delaware. :(
9. Does any national park contain an existential threat to modern civilization?
Yes. Yellowstone Caldera could destroy us all and there's nothing we can do to stop it!
10. The youngest national park:
Pinnacles National Park, in California. It was designated in 2013.

Guadalupe National Park

  • Humans have lived in this area for 10,000+ years
  • The highest point in Texas is Guadalupe Peak, sitting at 8,751 feet.
  • There are over 80 miles of trails in Guadalupe National Park
  • There are 16 different species of bats found in Guadalupe National Park.

Denali National Park and Preserve

  • Denali only has one road bisecting the entire park.
  • It takes about 3 weeks to hike to the top of Denali.
  • The name Denali stems from native Athabaskan languages, and can mean “the tall one” or “mountain-big.”
  • Park rangers utilize sled dogs.

Carlsbad Caverns National Park

  • Carlsbad is made up of at least 120 caves, and there's still more unexplored.
  • In the early 20th century, guano was mined here.
  • For two years after its designation, visitors had to enter the caves via guano bucket!
  • Lechuguilla Cave is at least 140 miles long and 1,600 feet deep.
